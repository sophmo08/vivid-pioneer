abstract: 'Women in Germany continue to earn an average of 18 percent less than men. Even when comparing men and women in identical jobs, this gap does not disappear — on average, women earn 6 percent less than men for the same work. This report examines why the gender pay gap persists and what organizational and political measures could help close it, drawing on salary data from over 1.7 million individuals.'
summary: 'In 2023, the gender pay gap in Germany stood at 18 percent — unchanged for the fifth year in a row. Even among full-time workers with the same education, employer, and hierarchical level, a gap of 6 percent remains. This report identifies four key mechanisms driving persistent pay inequality: unequal pay raises that widen the gap as careers progress, insufficient female representation on supervisory boards (with pay inequality only reduced once 50 percent parity is achieved), the moderating role of company diversity targets, and regional variation linked to childcare infrastructure. The findings point to concrete levers for change: binding pay policies, ambitious diversity targets, full gender parity on supervisory boards, and expanded childcare services — particularly in West Germany.'
